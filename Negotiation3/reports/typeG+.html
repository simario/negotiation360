Two questions you just answered deal with the interpersonal dimension of negotiation, specifically, asserting oneself and understanding others. Research show that most people feel that being relatively good at one of these skills comes at the cost of being less effective at the other. (Being firm about your demands could stress a relationship, for example. Or appearing cooperative might be misread as being weak.)

Two other questions on the test address outcomes. Here again, a trade-off is involved. People who feel they are good at one skill tend to think they are relatively weaker with the other. SpecifIcally, good value-creators often are less sure about their ability to get their share of the resulting pie (and conversely).

You can think of the interpersonal scale as representing the "means" of negotiation and the outcome scale as the "ends." Putting the two scales together reveals common bargaining styles.
