  • Two questions you answered deal with the interpersonal dimension of negotiation: asserting yourself and understanding others. Research show that many people feel that being relatively good at one of these skills comes at the cost of being less effective at the other. (Being firm about your demands could stress a relationship, for example; or appearing cooperative might be misread as weakness.) Nevertheless, some people feel equally good at both skills.

  • Two other questions address outcomes, specifically the ability to create value and capture your share of it. In practice there is often a tension between these two skills. Value-creating trades require understanding the parties' complementary interests. But if you disclose your interests unilaterally, you might be exploited by others who bluff and conceal their true needs. Value will be created, but they may get most of it.
