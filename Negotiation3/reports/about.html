Everyone negotiates. At work. In their communities. And even at home with their families. Negotiating well means:

  • reaching agreements that might slip through most people's fingers
  • creating value that others might overlook
  • resolving small differences before they become costly disputes

Successful negotiators are well-rounded. They rely on complementary interpersonal, analytic, and problem-solving skills. They also are agile. They adjust their approach to the situation at hand and deal with different bargaining styles.

To enhance your own effectiveness as a negotiator, start by honestly assessing your current strengths and weaknesses.
